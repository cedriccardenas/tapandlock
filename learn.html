Common Crimes around the Internet

Cyber Bullying

the electronic posting of mean-spirited messages about a person often done anonymously

Phishing

posing as a legitimate institution via email or contacts luring victims on giving away sensitive data such as passwords and banking details

Plagiarism

is presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own, with or without their consent, by incorporating it into your work without full acknowledgement

Cyber Stalking

the act of expressing or implying physical threats that creates fear through computer or network activities.

Misinformation

false information that is spread, regardless of whether there is intent to mislead

Online Scams

a confidence game or other fraudulent scheme, especially for making a quick profit

INFOGRAPHICS

Stranger Danger!

Be careful on who you meet online!

Tip #1 Be safe

-Have fun hanging out with people but be wary of giving out your information to others

Tip #2 Find Common interest

-It's always a good start to find people with the same interest and hobbies

Tip #3 Look into the right Places

- There are forums and anonymous chat rooms where you can talk to others without giving your identity. If you want to be in a place where everyone has a name, you can turn to a Facebook group to make friends online. Just make sure your profile has its privacy settings adjusted not to reveal any personal information.

Learn About Scams

BOILER ROOM

-A scheme that targets (usually) investors to purchase questionable investments
-Some notable tactics to look out for include making claims that cannot be easily verified by the investor, demanding immediate payment, or issuing threats for noncompliance.

LOVE/ROMANCE SCAM

-This scheme targets emotionally vulnerable people by making fake profiles on dating sites and social media
-Once you are in contact with a scammer, they will express strong emotions for you in a relatively short period of time and will suggest you move the relationship away from the website, to phone, email and/or instant messaging and urging you to provide money, gifts or personal details.

LOTTERY SCAM

-A scheme that involves a message from a lottery company out of the blue, they claim to tell you you've won and/or recieved a grand prize in a lottery/competition you didn't enter.
-The email, letter or text message you receive about your winnings will ask you to respond quickly or risk missing out. The scammers do this to try and stop you thinking about the surprise too much in case you start to suspect it could be a scam.

CARD SKIMMING

-It is the illegal copying of information from the magnetic strip of a credit or ATM card. The scammers try to steal your details so they can access your accounts.
-Once scammers have skimmed your card, they can create a fake or cloned card with your details on it.

INHERITANCE SCAM

-This scheme that involves a message from someone out of the blue claiming you're a benifactor or heir to a wealthy person who has supposedly died without a will
-Some scammers will provide a made-up name for your supposed relative. Others will use publicly available family history websites and gather the names of genuine deceased relatives to make the scam seem more convincing.

CHECK OVERPAYMENT SCAM

-A scheme that targets online sellers
-The scammer will be willing to buy your product/service and sends more money than the agreed price, then they'll ask you for a refund for the excess money only hoping for you to not notice that their check is bounced, leaving you with debt and probably loss of a product
